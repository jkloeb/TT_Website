Key Findings

Non-Patients show up because they think it’s safe, or it’s in the neighborhood, or they’re new citizens.

People who come cannot leave easily once the disaster is over and hospital employees often have to take them home themselves.

Hospitals resort to ad hoc methods to track non-patients.

There aren’t enough resources for special needs citizens in disasters.

Will It Help? (Evaluative Research)

User testing was conducted both on-site and remotely in Florida, as well as in Pittsburgh (using a severe winter storm as the test case instead of a hurricane). People with medical needs as well as caregivers for people with medical needs comprised the vast majority of our test subjects.

Key Findings

From user testing we learned that many elderly people are wary about giving their data to an app, that for some users coupons are greatly welcomed, whereas for others, they are a frivolous triviality, and that most users would want a clear explanation for why the app is recommending a specific course of action, or else, they would be unwilling to head it. All of these and more were taken into consideration as we further refined and iterated our app.
