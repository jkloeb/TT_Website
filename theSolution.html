The Mobile Application: Haven

The heart of the solution, Haven helps citizens prepare for disasters, both in terms of material and logistics. To this end, there are four core features that facilitate disaster readiness:

Onboarding
Preparation
Registration
Evacuation

Onboarding

The opening splash screen.

Onboarding

Getting people familiar with an app is essential to getting them to use it, especially in the case of more skeptical users, as indicated in our tests.

The onboarding process takes place as soon as the user first opens Haven. Haven's basic functions are explained in a clear and precise manner.

Onboarding

The new user is instructed to input their basic information. Haven does not draw information from the phone. This is a matter of trust, as per user testing.

Haven does, however, customize its functionality based who the user is: A caregiver, a citizen with special medical needs, or a person without special medical needs.

Preparation

Knowing who will accompany the user in the event of an evacuation allows Haven to suggest optimal preparation arrangements. For example, the preparation list for a user with pets will recommend obtaining additional items, such as pet food.

Preparation

Progress bars allow users to quickly scan how prepared they are and help to encourage completion.

The card layout was unanimously preferred over bullet points during user tests.

Preparation

The "i" icon brings up detailed information explaining why a particular item is needed in an emergency kit.

Preparation

Users said they were more likely to complete a request when they understood its neccesity.

Preparation

As the user completes more of their preparation the haven in the upper left corner becomes more and more secure.

Registration

Not every person who can register for a special needs shelter does, and of those that do, not many use them. They either aren’t aware of their availability, or they have trouble with the steps needed to sign up. Haven helps encourage registration by simplifying and streamlining the proccess.

Registration

Users can immediately see what kind of information they need to know to help spedcial medical needs shelters prepare for their intake, such as if they need assistance in an evacuation.

The blue button in the upper right of the screen allows users to easily share their information with family and friends if desired, or neccesary.

Registration

Detailed completion of a person's medical information also helps Haven determine whether a shelter has the facilities to their needs.

Evacuation

In case of evacuation, Haven recommends the best route to safety based on up-to-the-minute geographical and weather information.

Evacuation

This is Haven's recommendation for the best sheltering options one week before a storm.

Based on conditions and the medical and personal information of the user, or the person they care for Haven suggests the safest places to shelter. Again, clear explanaition of the reasons behind Haven's recommendations is key to encouraging follow-through.

Evacuation

As information changes, so does Haven's recommendations for shelter. Now it's 3 days before the storm and it is no longer recommended for the user to shelter-in-place, they are in danger of losing electricity.
